For our project, we decided to investigate the complex interplay between income disparities and personal choices regarding food and nutrition policies. Using a complete and extensive dataset, we will examine the different correlations that can be made between the consumers' diet / health and multiple outer factors, like income levels and food prices. Further numbers we will analyze include, for example, physical activity levels and drug use, to understand dietary habits and health outcomes. We will combine our findings and visualize them, with our goal being to inform on the intricate influences on everyday nutritional policies and personal choices regarding dieting and healthier lifestyles.

Regarding the perspectives we looked at

Perspective 1: Income Disparities and Nutritional Inequality This perspective is about the impact of income inequality on nutritional inequality. We will analyze our data on income and food prices, and use this data to display how economic factors contribute to difference in access to certain types of food. Our aim is to explore any correlations between these two topics, and explore how these might come to be. This could shed light on the importance of economy on dietary habits, and could help with finding solutions to prominent health risks in today's society.

Perspective 2: Personal Responsibility and Education In our second perspective, we focus on the role of personal responsibility and education in shaping healthier dietary choices. For this perspective, we will need to make use of our other data regarding more personal choices, such as physical activity, drug use and education. With this information, we might find correlations between everyday habits and overall health outcomes. We will analyze these correlations and visualize them, which could help in finding effective methods of improving society's overall health with not just nutrition policies, but perhaps also through other means.
